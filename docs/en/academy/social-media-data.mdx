# Social Media Data Mining

> How teams collect public social posts, profiles, engagement, and trend signals while respecting platform limits, privacy, and API alternatives.

## What to Collect

| Platform | Data Type | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| Twitter/X | Posts, profiles, hashtags, trends | Brand monitoring, sentiment analysis |
| LinkedIn | Profiles, company pages, job postings | B2B lead generation |
| Instagram | Posts, profiles, hashtags | Influencer research |
| Reddit | Posts, comments, subreddits | Trend analysis, sentiment |

## Legal and Ethical Considerations

<Warning>
  Scraping social media raises significant legal and ethical concerns. Many platforms prohibit scraping in their ToS. Public data does not mean free to use. GDPR and CCPA apply to personal data. Consult legal counsel before large-scale social media scraping.
</Warning>

## Practical Approaches

1. **Use official APIs first** - Most platforms offer official APIs
2. **Scraping as fallback** - Use scraping only when APIs are insufficient
3. **Focus on public data** - Profile names, posts, engagement metrics
4. **Respect rate limits** - Slow, respectful scraping reduces risk
5. **Do not republish** - Use scraped data for analysis, not redistribution
