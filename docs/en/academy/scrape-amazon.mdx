# How to Scrape Amazon

> A practical guide to extracting Amazon product data: search results, product pages, reviews, variants, prices, seller data, and anti-bot considerations.

Amazon is one of the most valuable and most difficult e-commerce sources to scrape. Product pages contain price, seller, reviews, variants, rankings, images, and specifications, but the site is dynamic, heavily monitored, and different marketplaces can expose different layouts.

## Page Types

| Page type | Best for | Typical fields |
|---|---|---|
| Search results | Product discovery and ranking | Title, URL, image, price, rating, review count, ASIN |
| Category pages | Category research | Product cards, rank position, category URL |
| Product detail pages | Deep product intelligence | Brand, ASIN, price, seller, bullets, description, specs, variants |
| Review pages | Sentiment and quality analysis | Review title, rating, text, date, reviewer, verified purchase |
| Seller pages | Marketplace monitoring | Seller name, storefront URL, seller rating |

## Step 1: Pick the Input

Use **keywords** when you need discovery:
```
wireless keyboard
protein powder
kids winter boots
```

Use **product URLs or ASINs** when you already know the catalog:
```
https://www.amazon.com/dp/B08...
B08...
```

For price monitoring, ASINs or product URLs are usually better than repeating keyword searches.

## Step 2: Collect Listing Data

Search and category pages are useful for breadth. Capture:
- Product title
- Product URL and ASIN
- Price
- Rating and review count
- Image URL
- Sponsored/organic signal
- Result position
- Timestamp

<Note>
  The result position matters. A product's rank for a keyword can be as important as its price.
</Note>

## Step 3: Visit Product Detail Pages

Detail pages provide fields needed for serious analysis:
- Brand and feature bullets
- Product description and specifications
- Variants
- Seller and fulfillment signals
- Best Sellers Rank
- Full image set
- Coupon or promotion signals

## Step 4: Scrape Reviews Separately

Review extraction is its own workflow. Sort order, pagination, language, star filters, and region can change the output.

Useful review fields: rating, review title, review text, date, reviewer name, verified purchase flag, helpful vote count, product variant.

## Technical Challenges

Amazon scraping commonly runs into:
- Dynamic layouts and A/B tests
- Regional price and availability differences
- CAPTCHA and bot-detection challenges
- Variant-specific fields
- Missing or hidden data
