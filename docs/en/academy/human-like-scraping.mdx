# Human-Like Scraping

> Even a clean fingerprint gets a scraper flagged if the mouse moves in straight lines. The behavioral tells that mark a scraper as a bot, and how to remove them.

A scraper that runs in a real headed browser with a managed fingerprint has handled the "what does this session look like" layer. But modern anti-bot systems also watch what the visitor does once inside: how the mouse moves, where it clicks, how fast it types, whether it scrolls before reading.

## What Machines Do That Humans Dont

- **Mouse moves in straight lines** - Real cursors curve, jitter, overshoot, and correct. Bots typically interpolate a straight path.
- **Clicks land on exact center pixels** - Humans aim at a button, they do not hit dead-center every time.
- **Timing is constant** - sleep(2) between every action looks like exactly what it is. Real users vary.
- **No scrolling, no dwell** - A bot fetches what it needs and leaves. A human scrolls, hesitates, sometimes scrolls back.
- **Navigation is too efficient** - A bot follows the shortest path. A human wanders.

## Designing Human-Like Behavior

<Steps>
  <Step title="Mouse trajectories">
    Replace linear moveTo calls with Bezier or noise-perturbed paths that arc, jitter, and occasionally overshoot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click offset">
    Click slightly off-center within the target element bounding box.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Timing distributions">
    Replace constant sleeps with samples from a distribution (log-normal works well) so action intervals look organic.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scroll and dwell">
    Insert scroll events with pauses; let some time pass on a page even when the data you want is already in the DOM.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Imperfect navigation">
    Occasionally click a non-target link, then back-navigate; visit a few peer pages before the one you came for.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## When It Matters, When It Doesnt

| Site Defense Level | Approach |
|---|---|
| Light (static sites) | Skip it. A clean headed browser is enough. |
| Medium (rate limiting + basic detection) | Do timing distributions, scroll, mouse curves. |
| Heavy (Cloudflare, DataDome, Akamai) | Required. Without realistic behavior you are paying for rendering and still getting blocked. |
