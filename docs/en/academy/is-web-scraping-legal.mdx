# Is Web Scraping Legal?

> Web scraping legality depends on what you scrape, how, and where. An overview of public data, ToS, copyright, GDPR/CCPA, and how to scrape compliantly.

## Key Legal Considerations

### 1. Public vs. Private Data

**Public data** (visible without login) is generally less risky. Private data (behind login or paywall) introduces additional legal concerns.

### 2. Terms of Service

Most websites prohibit scraping in their ToS. Courts have reached different conclusions depending on jurisdiction.

### 3. Copyright

The structure and creative expression of a website may be copyrighted. Facts are not copyrightable - scraping prices and contact info is generally safer.

### 4. GDPR and CCPA

Scraping personal data of EU or California residents may make you a data controller with associated obligations.

## Practical Risk Mitigation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Scrape public data when possible">
    Prioritize publicly accessible pages over authenticated ones.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the ToS carefully">
    Understand what you are agreeing to before scraping.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Respect robots.txt">
    Demonstrates good faith even if not legally binding everywhere.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rate limit your requests">
    Do not overload the target server.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Minimize data collection">
    Only collect the data you actually need.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Consult a lawyer">
    For commercial or high-risk projects, get legal advice.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Factor | Risk Level |
|---|---|
| Public factual data | Low |
| Public content (large scale) | Medium |
| ToS-prohibited scraping | Medium-High |
| Private/authenticated data | High |
| Personal data (GDPR/CCPA) | High |

_This is general legal information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney._
