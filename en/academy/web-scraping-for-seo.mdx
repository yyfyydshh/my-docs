# Web Scraping for SEO

> How to use web scraping for SEO research, competitor analysis, search optimization, and search rank tracking.

## What to Collect

| Data Type | Use Case |
|---|---|
| Competitor content | Analyze what topics and formats competitors rank for |
| Keyword rankings | Track search rankings across keywords and locations |
| Backlink profiles | Monitor new and lost backlinks |
| Site structure | Map site architecture and identify crawlability issues |
| On-page elements | Collect title tags, H1s, meta descriptions, and content length |

## Common Workflows

### Competitor Content Analysis

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify competitors">
    Find the sites that rank for your target keywords.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collect their content">
    Scrape titles, descriptions, headings, and content from their top pages.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Analyze patterns">
    Identify content length, keyword usage, format, and structure patterns.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply insights">
    Use findings to inform your own content strategy.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Rank Tracking

Track where your pages and competitor pages rank for target keywords across search engines and locations.

### Technical SEO Auditing

- Find broken links and 404 pages
- Identify duplicate or thin content
- Map site structure and internal linking
- Collect title tags, meta descriptions, and heading structures

## Tools and Approaches

- **Rank trackers** - SEMrush, Ahrefs, Moz for tracking rankings (not scraping)
- **Site crawlers** - Screaming Frog, Sitebulb for technical SEO analysis
- **Custom scrapers** - For bespoke competitor research beyond standard tools

## Important Notes

- Respect robots.txt directives for SEO crawlers
- Do not scrape Google directly - use official search APIs for rank data
- Focus on adding value with your analysis, not just copying competitor content
