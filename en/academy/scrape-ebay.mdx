# How to Scrape eBay

> How to extract product listings, seller data, prices, reviews, and auction data from eBay for competitive intelligence, price monitoring, and market research.

## What to Collect

| Data Type | Fields |
|---|---|
| Product listings | Title, price, condition, format (auction/buy-it-now), location |
| Seller data | Username, feedback score, location, member since |
| Auction data | Current bid, bid count, time left, buy-it-now price |
| Search results | All matching items with prices and seller info |

## Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start from search results">
    Begin with category or keyword search URLs to collect broad listings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Extract listing data">
    Collect title, current price, condition, format, and seller information.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Visit detail pages">
    Extract full descriptions, specifications, shipping options, and review data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Handle pagination">
    eBay search results paginate; collect all listings relevant to your research.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Technical Notes

- eBay uses JavaScript rendering; a real browser is required for reliable extraction.
- Pagination uses URL parameters like `?_pgn=` or follows "Next" links.
- Seller feedback scores can be extracted from listing pages or seller profile pages.
- Monitor for rate limits on high-volume collection.

## Common Use Cases

- **Price monitoring** - Track sold and current prices across categories
- **Competitor research** - Monitor what sellers are offering and at what price
- **Auction intelligence** - Track bid patterns and sell-through rates
- **Product research** - Validate demand for products before sourcing
