# AI vs Traditional Web Scraping

> When AI-driven scraping beats selector-based scraping, when it does not, and how to combine the two.

## Two Approaches

**Traditional (Selector-Based) Scraping** relies on manually defined XPath or CSS selectors. You inspect a page, write a selector, and hope the page structure stays the same.

**AI-Powered Scraping** uses machine learning to understand page structure semantically. Instead of saying "click the third div with class product-title", you say "extract the product name".

## Comparison

| Factor | Traditional | AI Scraping |
|---|---|---|
| Setup Time | Minutes to hours | Seconds to minutes |
| Maintenance | High - selectors break | Lower - AI adapts |
| Cost | Low | Higher (ML inference) |
| Complex Layouts | Requires expert writing | Easier |
| Batch Speed | Very fast | Moderate |

## When AI Scraping Wins

- Rapid prototyping - get data in minutes, not hours
- High-variance layouts - pages that differ across categories
- Low-maintenance needs - when you cannot monitor selectors
- Non-technical users - no XPath/CSS knowledge required

## When Traditional Scraping Wins

- Stable, predictable pages - government or academic sites
- Massive scale - millions of pages where speed matters
- Exact field requirements - precise control over what is extracted
- Cost-sensitive projects - no per-page inference cost

## Best Practice: Combine Both
